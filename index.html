Pest Control Strategies

Effective pest management using integrated approaches including biological, organic, and chemical methods.

Biological Control

Use natural predators and beneficial insects to control pests.

  • Ladybugs for aphids
  • Parasitoid wasps
  • Natural enemies
Organic Remedies

Eco-friendly methods that do not harm the environment.

  • Neem oil spray
  • Garlic-chili solution
  • Compost-based treatments
Chemical Control

Use pesticides carefully when infestation is severe.

  • Use approved pesticides
  • Follow dosage instructions
  • Ensure safety precautions

Integrated Pest Management (IPM)

IPM is a comprehensive approach that combines biological, organic, and chemical methods to control pests effectively while minimizing environmental damage.

Prevention
  • Use resistant crop varieties
  • Maintain soil health
  • Proper irrigation
Monitoring
  • Regular field inspection
  • Use pest traps
  • Early symptom detection
Control
  • Apply biological methods first
  • Use organic remedies
  • Chemical as last option

Monitoring & Early Detection

  • Inspect crops regularly for signs of pests
  • Identify pest types at early stages
  • Take immediate action to prevent spread
  • Use modern tools like traps and sensors
